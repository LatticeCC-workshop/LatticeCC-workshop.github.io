Abstract

Lattices, long studied in mathematics, have become indispensable tools in modern communications and cryptography. In communications, lattice codes serve as fundamental tools for achieving channel capacity and enabling efficient error correction. In cryptography, lattice based post-quantum cryptographic (PQC) schemes can offer strong worst-case hardness guarantees and resilience against quantum attacks. The synergy between these two fields is increasingly vital as we seek robust and efficient solutions for quantum-safe cryptography.

LatticeCC aims to bridge the gap between lattice coding and lattice cryptography. The workshop will feature invited talks, tutorials, and community-contributed presentations that span topics such as recent advances in lattice coding, ciphertext compression, error correction in cryptographic systems.

Please visit the website for more details and submission information: https://latticecc-workshop.github.io/

Importance and Timeliness:

With NIST’s ongoing standardization of post-quantum cryptographic algorithms, lattice-based schemes (e.g., CRYSTALS-Kyber) are leading candidates due to their balance of security and efficiency. This workshop addresses critical gaps in understanding how coding-theoretic techniques enhance lattice cryptosystems, particularly in error resilience and bandwidth optimization.

Brief CVs of Organizers

  • Cong Ling is a Professor of Information Theory and Cryptography at Imperial College London. His research focuses on the study of lattices and their applications to coding and cryptography, as well as exploring their connections with number theory and quantum information.
  • Shanxiang Lyu received the Ph.D. degree from Imperial College London, U.K., in 2018. He is currently an Associate Professor with the College of Cyber Security, Jinan University, Guangzhou, China. He serves as the Director of the Laboratory of Quantum and Post-Quantum Cryptography at the same college. He is also a recipient of the Guangdong Provincial Excellent Young Talents Program. Dr. Lyu was awarded the 2021 CIE Information Theory Society Yong-Star Award. He has served as Organizing Chair, Technical Program Committee (TPC) Member, or Session Chair for flagship conferences including the IEEE Information Theory Workshop (ITW), the International Conference on Information Security and Cryptology (Inscrypt), IEEE GLOBECOM, and IEEE VTC. His recent research interests include lattice codes and cryptography.
  • Ling Liu received the PhD degree from the Department of Electrical and Electronic Engineering, Imperial College London, U.K., in 2015. He was a postdoctoral researcher at Imperial College London until June 2017. From September 2017, he worked at Huawei Technologies on 5G polar codes. He joined the College of Computer Science and Software Engineering, Shenzhen University, in July 2019, and in 2023, he moved to the Next-Generation Information Technology Research Center, Guangzhou Institute, Xidian University. He has published over 30 papers in journals and conferences including IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, IEEE Transactions on Communications, IEEE Transactions on Signal Processing, ISIT, and ITW. He has received several honors, including Outstanding Graduate of Nanjing University, Merit Student of Peking University, the Shenzhen “Peacock Plan” Award, and the Shenlong Talent Program. He is currently supported by research grants from the National Natural Science Foundation of China, the Guangdong Natural Science Foundation, a high-level talent startup project, and the Tencent Rhino-Bird Young Faculty Program. His research interests include channel and source coding, network information theory, lattice-based cryptography, and physical-layer security.
